Rossmore
Forest Park

 

Rossmore Forest Park is Monaghan's pride and joy, as it stands onn the edge of the town with acres and acres of forest. The main entrance to Rossmore Forest can be located on the Newbliss/Threemilehouse road. Only the buttresses to the castle walls and the entrance stairway of the Rossmore Castle now remain providing a viewing point to the surrounding countryside. Examples of a wedge tomb and a court tomb dating back to about 3000 – 1800 BC exist within the park. A gold ‘lunula’ or collar, dating from 1800 BC was discovered in the park around 1930 and is on display in the National Museum, Dublin. A walled garden was developed as a ‘formal garden’. Its main attraction is the yew hedges and arched walkways of trained shrubs. Amongst its large number of trees and plant life are Californian redwoods planted at various locations. One of these Redwoods is situated near the entrance gate. The park also has an adjoining 18 hole golf course of Rossmore Golf Club.

Rossmore Castle

The building was a 19th-century castle. Originally built by the 2nd Lord Rossmore in 1827 in the Gothic style, it was extended in 1858. Unfortunately, after the Second World War, dry rot forced the abandonment of the castle, which has since been demolished.

Rossmore Mausoleum

The Rossmore Mausoleum was designed by E.J. Tarver by commission of the fifth Baron Rossmore. Much like the rossmore memorial in the Diamond, Monaghan Town, it commemorates the death of the fourth baron who died after a hunting accident at Windsor Castle in 1874, aged 23. Built on a rocky outcrop over a small river and waterfall, the picturesque mausoleum fell into disrepair and was victim to theft and graffiti in the late twentieth century. It is currently being painstakingly conserved by the voluntary Rossmore Mausoleum Conservation Group.

 

'Shrooms'

The film 'Shrooms' was shot in Rossmore Forest Park over the period of seven weeks in 2007. This is the first film shot in Rossmore Forest Park. Shrooms is a thriller/horror, and locals may even recognise parts of Rossmore Forest in the film.
